Why Bali?

Bali

Indonesia is a country of thousands of islands and while Bali is considered an island, it's also a province with a few smaller islands in its territory.. Bali's waters are teeming with marine life with at least 2,000 species of fish and other creatures including coral reefs, dolphins, turtles and much more. This is due to the fact that Bali sits in the centre of the Coral Triangle, the world's centre of marine biodiversity, also dubbed "the Amazon of the seas".

Bali has two active volcanoes; Mount Batur standing at over 1,700 metres tall and Mount Agung which is 3,000 metres tall. Hiking up these mountains will give you a stunning view of the island that no Bali souvenir can replace. Besides white beaches, Bali has multiple beaches with black sand as well. Thanks to the island's two active volcanoes, you can have a one-of-a-kind experience at black sand volcanic beaches such as Lovina and Amed Beach during your Bali trip.
